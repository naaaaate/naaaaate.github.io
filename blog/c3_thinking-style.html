Thinking Style

I think, therefore, I am.

3/15/15

This week I was asked to write a blog on my thoughts about DBC: my struggles so far, my thinking style, and how I can maybe use some of that info to my advantage in this program.
After taking the personal thinking style quiz, (which you can find here: Thinking Style Quiz ), the results show that my style is a: "Concrete Sequential" (CS). A few characteristics of CS's are: they are known to like order, logical sequence, predictability, facts, and following directions, just to name a few. These characteristics describe me to the T! It's also said that CS's learn best when in a structured environment and can rely on others to complete a task. I feel I can use my understanding of my thinking style to my advantage because DBC provides such a structured environment to learn from and I feel that bc people in my cohort are very similar in their transition to a new career like myself, I can therefore rely on others to complete some of the same tasks that I am aiming for.


Now, with that being said, there is always two sides to a coin. I must say that even thought DBC is structured very well, in these first 3 weeks there have been times that I have struggled to learn some of the material.I think the biggest reason for that is because of the limited time I have with working 40+ hrs a week and staying up late trying to finish challenges on time. At times I feel a little rushed at times when it comes to learning the material, such as the web design and ruby code, bc althought the DBC program is structured well, the sites I navigate to on google to learn the actual material for the challenges being presented are not always ordered or in a logical sequence. Therefore being a CS is hard at times, bc I know that just about every day I will be faced with an unpredicatable situation and if you add that on top of the little time I already have to work with, it makes things just that much tougher. But no excuses..


One of the things I'm constantly reminded of at DBC and even from some of my peers is that I need to stay focused on a growth mindset. A growth mindset basically sees failure as an opportunity to grow and get better at that obstacle that has stopped you in your place. A fixed mindset on the other hand says because you have failed, you are not cut out for this career path. ie: Because you didnt know how to complete that challenge like the others in your group did, your not going to make it as a developer. And that ideaology could not be further from the truth. If anything, it pushes me and motivates me to get better and focus on areas that I am weak at in coding. I think the key to getting better is continued practice and reaching out for help when I need it the most. A lot of times I've found that just by simply picking the brains of my fellow co-horts has helped me learn concepts that they can explain to me in a more relevant way than a book or someone typing a random blog ever could. I think taking this plan of action is what will make me a more well rounded person, which will hopefully translate into learning more efficiently in the future.
Thanks,
~Nate
